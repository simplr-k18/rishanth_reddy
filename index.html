About

I believe great engineering starts with a simple question: What decision is this person trying to make?

Over the past 5 years, I've built data systems processing billions of records across banking and global procurement. But the work I'm most proud of isn't the scale-it's the clarity. The 100+ hours per week saved because teams no longer hunt for data. The 80% improvement in data quality because we changed how we thought about the problem, not just how we coded the solution.

At Baxter International, I built the company's first unified data product by starting with the fundamental problem: procurement teams across 4 continents couldn't trust their own data. I didn't just integrate 10+ systems-I interviewed stakeholders, understood their workflows, and designed a product that 200+ people now rely on daily. At First Citizens Bank, I redesigned core banking architecture by asking: what if we organized data around how people use it, not how systems store it? The result: Customer 360 and 5 other data products that became the foundation for enterprise decision-making.

My approach combines three principles:

1. Start with the problem, not the technology - I interview users, map workflows, understand pain points before writing code. Sometimes the best solution isn't more data-it's better clarity.

2. Build for humans, then optimize for systems - I taught myself Figma to design better interfaces. I create custom dashboards that follow design systems. I believe tools should feel intuitive, not intimidating.

3. Measure what matters - Not lines of code or query performance alone, but: Did this save time? Did this improve decisions? Do people actually want to use this?

This philosophy has led me to create procurement taxonomies (interviewing 30+ stakeholders), build custom internal tools (HTML/CSS/JS, self-taught), and design data products that teams across finance, operations, and C-suite rely on. I work at the intersection of engineering rigor and human-centered design.

>> Currently exploring: How AI can help procurement teams make better sourcing decisions faster-not by replacing human judgment, but by augmenting it with better information at the right moment.

Work

Baxter International | Senior Analytical Engineer & Data Architect
(Aug 2023 – Present | Bangalore, India)
The story began with procurement teams working from different systems, different assumptions, and different truths. My role was to stop the noise and design a single product that made trust inevitable.

I led Baxter’s first enterprise spend product from discovery through launch. Instead of starting with the data source, I started with the decision: what does a buyer and a finance leader need to know in the next 10 minutes?

The result is a unified analytics experience built on Starburst and AWS S3, bringing together JDE, Coupa, SAP, Maximo and other procurement systems into a single queryable workflow. It was not just an engineering project — it was a product for teams who needed confidence, not more dashboards.

Alongside the product, I built tooling to automate reporting and surfaced supplier hierarchy rules so the business could act on clean data. This turned long, manual reporting cycles into a single trusted source for 20+ user groups.

What this meant in practice: decisions that used to take days now move in hours, supplier data quality improves from the ground up, and dozens of teams stop asking whether the data is “correct.”

Technologies: AWS (S3, Glue, Athena), Starburst, Power BI, Tableau, Python, SQL
First Citizens Bank (Silicon Valley Bank) | Data Architect & Modeling Lead
(Aug 2019 – Aug 2023 | Chennai, India)
My work at First Citizens was about turning a messy start into a reliable product. The first step was admitting that most of the pain came from trying to do analytics with spreadsheets and disconnected models.

I helped shift the team from one-off reports to reusable products: Customer 360, Investor 360, and credit data products that were built to be used, not just compiled. The focus was on designing data solutions around people, not systems.

One of the biggest wins came from changing how the business matched customers and legal entities. By rebuilding the matching hierarchy, we turned a chaotic process into an operational capability, reducing manual fix work by months of effort.

In the end, the work was no longer just about rows and reports. It became a layer of trusted, shared products that let sales, risk and operations move faster together.

Technologies: ER Studio, SQL, Python, Informatica, Airflow, AWS, Tableau
